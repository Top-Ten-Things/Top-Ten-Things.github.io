Stanford Online High School Hackathon 2022

In September 2022, Stanford OHS had a hackathon, a challenge where participants had to code an application or a website in 2 days. We coded a website called Health@Home which essentially provides over the counter medication recommendations for common ailments.

The user has to input their age, weight, and symptom(s). It then outputs an over-the-counter medicine recommendation. We researched many different medicines and common symptoms to create this website. Because of "its well chosen and surprisingly usable idea that has been overlooked in a well trodden segment," our project won a Special Recognition!

Pop The Lock

After coming from the arcade, my brother challenged me to code Pop The Lock so that he could practice in his free time and win the jackpot. Since almost all the part are circluar or involve circular motion, this was an extremely hard project. I tried many methods of making the red line spin, but all of them failed. A couple days later, I read about parametric equations and realized this would the perfect solution to my problem. Using a given value of t (a variable), I can find the x and y coordinates of the 2 points on the line. Plus, if I want to speed it up, all I have to do is increase t by a greater amount. After figuring that out, it was smooth sailing. This was one of my most challenging projects.
