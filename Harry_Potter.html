Broom

I have always been passionate about pushing the boundaries of my creativity. For this project, I used a walking stick we picked up on our neighborhood walk and experimented with different materials and color to design this perfect home-made original broom.


Quidditch Mania



I have enjoyed reading and rereading the Harry Potter series and in fourth grade, I worked with my best friend and made a board game called Quidditch Mania. We both enjoyed playing our game online for hours during the pandemic.



Birthday

My goal for this project was to plan unique and creative Harry Potter themed birthday party games that we could all play as a group and have lots of fun. I used our backyard drawing to represent the Quidditch Field. I designed brooms using pool noodles and pom poms from the dollar store. The Hoola Hoops were a perfect fit for our goals. The second game for our birthday was Hit the demeter. We used water balloons to hit our target from a throwing line. We played in small groups and everyone had a great time!




Scratch Project


I started making this game a couple years ago when I was new to coding. It started out as a simple game where you would duel against another player. Then I added a home screen and the ability to change different variables like collision and spell speed. Next, I added the shop and a currency to the game. Finally, I added the spells and health potions. I plan on making a computer player next.


DIRECTIONS:

W/S = move Player 1 (Harry) up and down
Q = Player 1 (Harry) spell

↑ arrow/↓ arrow = move Player 2 (Ron) up and down
Space Bar = Player 2 (Ron) spell

If spells collide both players lose 1 health point
If spell hits you, you lose 1 health point

If you hit the head, it does a critical hit and takes two extra health points!
Win games to earn money to buy better spells and health potions.
