ASTROPHYSICS

Astrophotography App

I created a Python code that helps with astrophotography by stacking images to make them less blurry. The code was split into three parts: cropping, stacking, and enhancing.




Johnson Space Center


One of the perks of living in Houston is the regular visits to the Johnson Space Center. In 2019, I attended a week long winter camp at NASA where I learned about the Perseverance rover and the upcoming launch. We even got a guided tour for the Mars exhibit.






Delusions of Space Enthusiasts
2023

In March, I attended Neil deGrasse Tyson's talk in which he discussed common misconceptions about astronomy, like the vagueness of the Kármán line and the importance of Space Exploration. His sense of humor was one of my favorite things! Ever since I read his book Astrophysics for Young People in a Hurry in fourth grade, I have fallen in love with astrophysics and astronomy.


FORENSIC SCIENCE


CTY Summer Camp
2022

After being recognized with Johns Hopkins Center for Talented Youth's (CTY) Grand Honors Award, I had the opportunity to attend a fun 3 week residential summer camp at Ursinus College, PA. In my course, Examining the Evidence, we learned about the techniques forensic scientists use to analyze hair, fibers, fingerprints, impressions, and documents while processing a crime scene.


CHEMISTRY/BIOCHEMISTRY


3D Molecular Visualization

I have been interested in atoms and molecules since I was very little. In the Children's Museum in Houston, I played around with making different substances. Later in 5th grade, I took an online course called Biochemistry Literacy for Kids, which introduced me to more complex molecules like Buckminsterfullerene. It also taught me about PyMOL, a software to experiment connecting different atoms.
